Our Mission

The credible custodian of Zimbabwe's bamboo future

Bamboo Zimbabwe seeks formal appointment as National Bamboo Ambassador — the recognised focal partner for coordinating bamboo development, translating global knowledge into local impact, and driving investment and innovation that delivers tangible benefits for Zimbabwe's economy, environment, and communities.

Despite Zimbabwe's enormous bamboo potential — with confirmed indigenous species and dozens of naturalised varieties — the sector remains unstructured, underdeveloped, and without a dedicated national policy or coordinating body. We are changing that.

What we are appointed to do

Bamboo Zimbabwe respectfully proposes appointment by ICBR and INBAR as the National Bamboo Ambassador for Zimbabwe, with a mandate covering six strategic areas of work.

01
Central Coordinating Authority

Establish a central authority to guide and coordinate all national bamboo initiatives, serving as the go-to resource for government ministries, investors, and communities.

02
Primary International Liaison

Serve as the structured entry point for INBAR and ICBR collaboration — translating global bamboo knowledge and networks into Zimbabwean and SADC-wide impact.

03
Curated Pilot Projects

Lead demonstration projects proving bamboo's economic, environmental, and climate impact — from seedling nurseries and plantation management to value-addition enterprises.

04
Investment Mobilisation

Attract domestic and international capital for sector-wide scale-up. Produce transparent reporting to build investor confidence and attract carbon finance and green bonds.

05
Policy and Regulatory Advice

Advise ministries on bamboo policy frameworks, guidelines, and regulatory strategies. Facilitate multi-stakeholder dialogues while maintaining diplomatic respect for government mandates.

06
Monitoring and Reporting

Maintain rigorous baselines, monitor progress against sector targets, and publish annual reports to ensure accountability to ICBR, INBAR, and the Government of Zimbabwe.

The ABC Model

Our three-phase strategic framework anchors bamboo as the centrepiece of Zimbabwe's green economic transformation.

Phase A
Foundation

Establishing the structural base — seedling production, plantation establishment, and technical protocol development for sustainable harvesting.

  • Nursery establishment and seedling production
  • Sustainable plantation management protocols
  • Technical harvesting standards
  • Propagation and quality management systems
Phase B
Value Addition

Unlocking economic value through processing, manufacturing, and technology adoption that creates jobs and competitive export products.

  • Furniture, panels, boards, and charcoal
  • Textiles, pulp, and bioenergy
  • Plastic substitute materials
  • Technology transfer and localisation
Phase C
Community & Environment

Delivering systemic social and environmental returns — rural livelihoods, youth empowerment, ecosystem restoration, and carbon finance.

  • Rural livelihood and income generation
  • Youth and women-led enterprise support
  • Afforestation and wetland restoration
  • Carbon sequestration and climate finance

Zimbabwe's Bamboo Species

Two confirmed indigenous species and over 30 naturalised varieties — a rich biological foundation for sector development.

Indigenous
African Bamboo
Oxytenanthera abyssinica

Zimbabwe's most widespread indigenous bamboo. Found along the Zambezi Valley and lowveld river systems. Used for construction, basket weaving, and traditional instruments.

Indigenous
Mountain Bamboo
Oreobambos buchwaldii

Highland forest species found in the Eastern Highlands. A confirmed native forming dense bamboo stands in montane forest ecosystems.

Naturalised
Common Bamboo
Bambusa vulgaris

The most widely cultivated bamboo globally. Highly versatile for construction, furniture, and pulp. Adapts well to Zimbabwe's subtropical climate zones.

Naturalised
Balcooa Bamboo
Bambusa balcooa

Large, sturdy species with high culm strength. Suitable for structural applications, engineered bamboo boards, and industrial processing.
